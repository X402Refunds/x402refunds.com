# Consulate Arbitration Rules v1.0

The Consulate Arbitration Rules establish a transparent, neutral, and technologically-native framework for resolving disputes between autonomous agents, AI systems, and enterprises.

## Scope of Application

### When These Rules Apply

These rules govern disputes where:
- ✅ All parties agreed to Consulate arbitration in writing
- ✅ Dispute arises from AI agent interactions or SLAs
- ✅ Claim value is determinable and verifiable

### Exclusions

These rules do NOT apply to:
- ❌ Criminal matters
- ❌ Disputes involving bodily harm
- ❌ Family law matters
- ❌ Matters prohibited from arbitration by law

## Dispute Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filing">
    **Claimant initiates** by submitting:
    - Dispute manifest (JSON-LD formatted)
    - Evidence bundle with cryptographic proofs
    - Filing fee (based on claim value)
    - Proof of arbitration agreement
    
    <Tip>
    **Technical Format:** See [Agent Integration Guide](/agent-integration-guide) for API details
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Notice">
    **Within 24 hours:**
    - Respondent receives automated notification
    - Notification includes claim summary and evidence
    - Delivery is cryptographically confirmed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Response">
    **Respondent deadline:**
    - 7 days for claims under $10,000
    - 14 days for claims $10,000–$100,000
    - 21 days for claims over $100,000
    
    <Warning>
    Failure to respond may result in default judgment
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Panel Assignment">
    **Judge panel assigned:**
    - 3 judges selected by algorithm
    - Based on expertise, reputation, and availability
    - Panel disclosed to both parties
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Deliberation">
    **Panel reviews:**
    - All evidence and submissions
    - Applicable law and contract terms
    - Past precedents in similar cases
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Award">
    **Final determination:**
    - Binding ruling issued
    - Cryptographically signed by panel
    - Published to blockchain for immutability
    - Enforceable under arbitration agreement
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Evidence Requirements

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Acceptable Evidence Types" icon="file">
    - API logs and monitoring data
    - SLA agreements and contracts
    - Email and communication records
    - Financial impact documentation
    - Technical audit reports
    - Expert opinions (verified)
    
    <Note>
    All evidence must include SHA-256 hash for integrity verification
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Chain of Custody" icon="link">
    Each piece of evidence must include:
    - Timestamp of creation
    - SHA-256 cryptographic hash
    - Submitter's digital signature
    - Access control log
    - Verification metadata
    
    ```json
    {
      "evidenceId": "ev_abc123",
      "sha256": "a3f7c2e8b9d1...",
      "timestamp": "2025-10-09T10:00:00Z",
      "submitter": "did:agent:acme-corp",
      "signature": "0x...",
      "url": "s3://evidence/logs.json"
    }
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Evidence Verification" icon="check">
    Consulate automatically verifies:
    - File integrity (hash matching)
    - Digital signatures
    - Timestamp authenticity
    - Source attribution
    - Format compliance
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Judge Selection

### Panel Composition

- **3 judges** for most disputes
- **5 judges** for claims over $500,000
- **1 judge** for expedited disputes under $5,000

### Selection Algorithm

Judges are selected based on:
1. **Expertise** - Relevant domain knowledge
2. **Reputation** - Historical ruling quality
3. **Availability** - Current caseload
4. **Neutrality** - No conflicts of interest
5. **Geographic diversity** - Multiple jurisdictions

<Tip>
Judge selection is algorithmically randomized to prevent manipulation while optimizing for expertise.
</Tip>

## Precedent System

<Info>
Consulate maintains a searchable database of all past rulings to ensure consistency and predictability.
</Info>

Judges consider:
- **Similar fact patterns** - Past cases with comparable circumstances
- **Binding precedents** - Mandatory authority in jurisdiction
- **Persuasive precedents** - Guidance from related cases
- **Dissenting opinions** - Alternative viewpoints

## Confidentiality

<Warning>
**Default:** All filings and awards are **public** to ensure transparency.

**Confidential proceedings** are available upon mutual agreement, with higher fees.
</Warning>

### Public Information

- Case filings and responses
- Evidence (unless sealed by court order)
- Final awards and remedies
- Judge panel composition
- Procedural timeline

### Protected Information

- Settlement negotiations (always confidential)
- Personal identifying information
- Trade secrets (if properly designated)
- Information under protective order
